Chapter 4

Distance: Affairs Aren't Just About Sex

Emotional boundaries are essential in protecting your heart, honoring your spouse, and strengthening your marriage. This chapter will help each of you recognize how you give and receive emotionally, where your boundaries may be too loose or too rigid, and where they can be strengthened through Christ-centered growth.

Assessment Instructions
How to Use This Chapter
Step 1 — Complete the Assessment Individually

Each spouse should complete the assessment on their own, without discussing answers first. Find a quiet space where you can reflect honestly before God. Do not answer based on what you think your spouse wants to hear — answer based on your true thoughts, feelings, and habits.

Important: If you are not completely honest, the results will not serve you. The purpose is not to get the "perfect score," but to reveal areas where God may want to bring healing, correction, or encouragement in your relationship.
Step 2 — Pray Before You Begin

Pray individually, asking God to reveal truth in your heart, guard against defensiveness or pride, and help you see clearly where boundaries need to be established or repaired.

Self-Check
Emotional Boundaries Assessment — Circle YES or NO

Answer honestly. If you circled YES to more than one, it's a warning sign of emotional distance.

Key Idea
Recognizing the Danger of Distance

Distance in marriage opens the door to emotional or physical affairs. Affairs are not always sexual — they often begin with misplaced emotional intimacy.

Chapter 6

Deception: Romance vs. Reality — The 5 R's

Culture sells us a refund-style, receipt-based view of love. God calls us to a covenant — not convenience, not casual, but commitment that chooses to love through reality, ruts, resentment, and regret, and climbs back to oneness.

Fill in the Blanks
Key Truths from This Chapter
1. Covenants are about _______________ (not convenience).
2. God's expectation is: ___________, ___________, and watch Him do the ___________.
3. If I don't make an intentional choice to love, I end up with a ___________ and no ___________.
4. When I have entire conversations in my ___________ for my spouse, we never talk about the ___________.
The 5 R's
Map the Descent & Choose the Climb

We often drift down these steps without realizing it. Read each stage together, then mark where you feel your marriage is today:

  • Romance — Idealized expectations, fairy-tale scripts
  • Reality — Different doesn't mean "bad," just different
  • Rut — Apathy, auto-pilot, mind-reading instead of conversation
  • Resentment — Bitterness, blame, "civil war" triggers
  • Regret — Resignation, escape fantasies, affair risks (emotional/physical)

Step 2 — Individual Reflection

Each spouse should take a moment alone to answer. Write down your answers before sharing.

Which stage best describes where I feel we are in our marriage today?
What thoughts, feelings, or habits show me that we are at this stage?
What is one area where I see myself contributing to this drift?

Come together and share your answers. Use active listening: do not interrupt or defend. Simply listen and receive what your spouse says.

For the stage you identified, consider these climb steps:

  • Romance — Ground your expectations in truth; replace fantasy with gratitude for real blessings.
  • Reality — Embrace differences as opportunities to grow; practice curiosity instead of criticism.
  • Rut — Break the cycle by starting new conversations; schedule intentional connection time.
  • Resentment — Confess hurts, forgive, and reset; commit to resolving conflict instead of fueling it.
  • Regret — Seek help early (pastor, counselor, mentor couple); rebuild hope through prayer and action.
As a couple, write down one specific action you will take this week to climb upward from your current stage:
